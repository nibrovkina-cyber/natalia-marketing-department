# Schwartz awareness levels: 5 levels that change everything in copywriting

Copy sells to a cold prospect differently than a hot one. Obvious. But how exactly — not obvious.

Eugene Schwartz wrote a book about this in 1966 — Breakthrough Advertising. Since then, direct response exists thanks to his five awareness levels.

I apply this model in every skill for marketing in natalia-marketing-department. And in every text I write by hand.

In this article: five levels with examples, how to identify them in your audience, and what copy works at each.

---

## What awareness level is

An awareness level is **what the audience knows about their problem and the solutions**.

Schwartz: "If you don't understand what level the audience is on — your text is at best ineffective. At worst — repels."

Five levels:

1. **Most aware** — knows the product, ready to buy
2. **Product aware** — knows the product exists, not sure if it fits
3. **Solution aware** — knows solutions exist, doesn't know who
4. **Problem aware** — knows the pain, doesn't know solutions
5. **Unaware** — doesn't know they have a problem

Each level needs its own copy, its own hook, its own CTA.

---

## Level 1 — Most Aware (ready to buy)

### Who
Person already knows your product. Was on the site. Knows the price. Returns.

### What to write
Only offer and urgency. No explanations of why the product is needed.

❌ "What our AI marketing is and why it's better"
✅ "30% off until midnight. Today only."

### Channel
- Email retargeting (opened email but didn't buy)
- VK Ads remarketing (visited payment page)
- Yandex Direct on brand
- SMS to clients

### Length
Short. 30-60 words max. One CTA.

---

## Level 2 — Product Aware (knows product, unsure)

### Who
Heard about your product. Maybe saw an ad once. Not sure it's the solution.

### What to write
Comparisons, proof, testimonials. Close objections.

❌ "What is marketing"
✅ "Why our platform beats Geekbrains for AI marketing — comparison on 7 points"

### Channel
- Landing with FAQ
- Telegram channel with breakdowns
- Client cases on the site

### Length
Medium. 500-1500 words. Structure: objection → answer → proof.

---

## Level 3 — Solution Aware (looking for solution)

### Who
Knows AI marketers exist. Doesn't know who the good ones are. Comparing options.

### What to write
Content marketing. They should find you when they search.

❌ "Buy our subscription"
✅ "How to choose an AI tool for small business marketing in 2026: comparison of 7 platforms"

### Channel
- SEO articles on industry sites
- YouTube long-form
- Podcasts about AI

### Length
Long. 1500-3000 words. Educational.

### Keywords
"which AI marketing to choose", "comparison of neural networks for business", "best AI for copywriting"

---

## Level 4 — Problem Aware (has pain, no solution)

### Who
Feels the problem — no time for marketing, no budget for a marketer, content doesn't work. **Doesn't know AI solves this**.

### What to write
Name the pain precisely. Show the cause is not in them — in the tool.

❌ "AI for marketing"
✅ "Why you do marketing yourself and still get no clients. And what to do about it."

### Channel
- TikTok / VK Video — short format for broad audience
- Reddit r/Entrepreneur, r/SmallBusiness
- Telegram channels about small business

### Length
Emotional. 300-800 words. Story + turn + solution.

### Keywords
"marketing for small business", "not enough clients", "no time for marketing"

---

## Level 5 — Unaware (doesn't realize they have a pain)

### Who
Just lives. Maybe started a business recently. Hasn't thought of marketing as a systemic problem.

### What to write
**Stories**. Not selling. Not explaining. Content should catch off-guard.

❌ "Marketing is important for business"
✅ "This dentist thought he had good marketing. Until he compared with the neighbor around the corner."

### Channel
- YouTube long-form (10+ minutes)
- Storytelling videos
- Blogs with personal stories
- Podcasts

### Length
Long. Story without explicit offer. Sells the fact that the problem exists.

## How to identify audience level

### Question 1 — Where did they come from?

| Source | Level |
|---|---|
| Direct brand search in Yandex | Most Aware (4-5) |
| Search "buy [your product]" | Product Aware (3-4) |
| Search "best [category]" | Solution Aware (3) |
| Search "how to solve [problem]" | Problem Aware (2) |
| Random click from TikTok / Reels | Unaware (1) |

### Question 2 — What are they searching?

Open Yandex Wordstat for your keyword. Look at **related queries**.

If people search "[keyword] price" → they are Product Aware.
If "[keyword] vs [competitor]" → Solution Aware.
If "what is [keyword]" → Problem Aware.
If "[pain]" without product mention → Unaware.

### Question 3 — How do they talk?

Listen to how clients describe the problem on sales calls:

> "I need an AI marketer" → Solution Aware. Knows the category.
> "No time for posts" → Problem Aware. Doesn't know this is solvable.
> "I'm okay, just not enough leads" → Unaware. Doesn't connect cause and effect.

## Budget allocation by level

Schwartz taught: **80% of sales come from Most/Product Aware. 80% of content should target Problem/Solution Aware**.

Explanation: those ready to buy are the narrow neck of the funnel. The majority of the audience is Problem Aware. Content must **warm up bottom-up**.

### Practice for small business

| Level | % of audience | % of your content |
|---|---|---|
| Most Aware | 1-3% | 5% (email retargeting, remarketing) |
| Product Aware | 5-10% | 15% (FAQ, comparisons, testimonials) |
| Solution Aware | 15-20% | 35% (SEO articles, YouTube long-form) |
| Problem Aware | 30-40% | 30% (short stories, social) |
| Unaware | 30-50% | 15% (long-form storytelling, podcasts) |

## FAQ

**What if the audience is mixed?**
Any audience is mixed. Write for the average level + give anchors for extremes. For example, in an SEO article 80% content for Solution Aware, last paragraph with offer — for Most Aware.

**How is Schwartz related to Ogilvy?**
Ogilvy — about headlines. Schwartz — about which headlines work when. Ogilvy says "5 headline variants". Schwartz says "for each level — its own headline".

**Can I make one landing for all levels?**
No. A landing works for one level. If you make a "universal" one — it works for none. That's why people make separate landing pages for ad campaigns to different levels.

**Where to read Schwartz himself?**
"Breakthrough Advertising" — original. Tough book, written in 1966. Price: ~$150-300 on Amazon (limited print). Free summaries on Habr and other forums.

**How long does it take to identify the level?**
5-15 minutes per campaign. Done once at the start — then writing is fast because you know who you write for.
